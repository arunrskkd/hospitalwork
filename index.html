Irritable Bowel Syndrome

Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) is a common condition, associated with a range of symptoms including abdominal cramping, bloating, diarrhoea and constipation

Inflammatory Bowel Syndrome

Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) is a condition that involves chronic inflammation in all or part of the digestive tract. Often painful, IBD can lead to life-threatening complications as well as increase the risk for colon cancer.

Pancreatic Disorders

The pancreas plays an important role in maintaining homeostasis where it produces several digestive enzymes and the hormones that are necessary for controlling blood glucose levels. Any harm or injury to the pancreas may cause several problems.

Gastritis

Gastritis is the inflammation or irritation of the lining of the stomach. Some of the common symptoms are indigestion, burning pain in the upper abdomen and vomiting.

Gallstone Surgery

Gallstones can lead to pain in the upper right abdomen. In some cases, the doctor may need to perform a surgery that requires general anaesthesia.

Hernia Surgery

Most hernias are not life-threatening. But sometimes, they don't go away on their own and a surgery is required to avoid potentially dangerous complications.

Colectomy

: Surgery is one of the more common colorectal cancer treatment. The procedure may involve removing tumors, a section of the colon and in rate cases the entire colon may even be removed.
